What is Littering?

Littering is the act of improperly disposing waste products or trash, such as on the streets or sidewalks. Examples of littering include food containers, bottles, napkins, and cigarette butts, which comprises 50% of littering in the world. image showing examples of littering (maybe some photos by self)

Difference between littering and dumping

Littering is where small bits of garbage are disposed on roads or sidewalks. Unintentional littering occurs when garbage falls out of improperly secured vehicles or from overflowing dumpsters. Dumping, on the other hand, is the disposal of large amount of trash illegally (such as in empty lots instead of in a dumpster). Dumping also includes the disposal of hazardous material improperly.

Littering Stats

  • Philadelphia was ranked 3rd in the US for most roadside litter, after San Francisco and Los Angeles.
  • According to Philadelphia's Litter Index, there are around 10,000 blocks in the city with moderate to severe littering.
